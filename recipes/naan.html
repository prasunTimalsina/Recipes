Naan

Description

This is an authentic Indian naan recipe that's broiled instead of pan-fried. I have made it many times. It goes well with Indian curry dishes that have a lot of gravy, such as butter chicken.

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup warm water (110°F/45°C)
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup ghee
  • 2 teaspoons kalonji (onion seed)

Steps to follow

  1. Whisk warm water with yeast and sugar until yeast is dissolved. Cover and let stand in a warm place for 10 minutes.
  2. Sift flour and salt three times into a large bowl.
  3. Add yeast mixture, yogurt, and half of the ghee to the flour. Mix into a soft dough.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth and elastic (about 5 minutes).
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise in a warm place for 1.5 hours or until doubled in volume.
  6. Punch down the dough and knead for another 5 minutes.
  7. Divide the dough into six equal pieces.
  8. Roll each piece out into 8-inch rounds.
  9. Cover an oven tray with foil, grease it, and brush the dough rounds with the remaining ghee.
  10. Sprinkle kalonji (onion seed) on top.
  11. Cook each round under the broiler until puffed and lightly browned (about 2 minutes per side).
